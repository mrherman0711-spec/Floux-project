# Senior CMO Knowledge Base — Floux
## Load before any marketing task. Think, don't just audit.

---

## HOW TO THINK LIKE A CMO
Before any tactic ask: Who exactly? What awareness level? What's the ONE job? What does inaction cost? Why won't they buy? What category do we own?
**Rule:** Message-market fit before product-market fit. "Recuperamos las llamadas perdidas" IS the message. Every tactic reinforces it.

---

## THE 30 FRAMEWORKS (condensed)

**1. SCHWARTZ — Awareness Levels:** Floux owners are Problem Aware ("I miss calls") but NOT Solution Aware. Open every message at the pain level, then introduce the solution. Never assume they're ready to buy.

**2. HORMOZI — Value Equation:** Value = (Dream Outcome × Likelihood) / (Time × Effort). Don't sell "missed call recovery" — sell "15 clientes más al mes." Zero-effort positioning ("tú no haces nada") is Floux's strongest lever.

**3. OGILVY:** 5x more people read the headline than the body. Specifics sell. "15 clientes/mes" beats "muchos clientes." Every touchpoint must reinforce the same brand image.

**4. HOPKINS — Scientific Advertising:** Advertising is salesmanship. Test everything. Headlines do 80% of the work. Specific claim + reason to act now = "Alta gratuita solo durante el lanzamiento."

**5. HALBERT:** Find the starving crowd first (salons with most missed calls), then write copy. Specificity = credibility: "12 salones en Majadahonda" > "muchos salones."

**6. BRUNSON — Hook/Story/Offer:** Hook stops scroll → Story creates belief → Offer solves the problem the story introduced. If funnel fails, it's always one of these three. Floux hook: "Mientras cortas el pelo, tu competencia recoge tus clientes."

**7. KENNEDY — Marketing Triangle:** Message + Market + Media must align. Niche = riches. Direct CTA with deadline always beats open-ended offers. Floux USP: "El único sistema que convierte llamadas perdidas en citas — sin que tú hagas nada. En 48h."

**8. GODIN — Purple Cow:** "Very good" is invisible. Target early-adopter salon owners who will evangelize. The smallest viable market: 10 salons in Majadahonda who love Floux and tell competitors.

**9. MILLER — StoryBrand (SB7):** The salon owner is the hero. Floux is the guide. Villain = the missed call. Plan = 3 steps max. Failure = client calls competitor (€1,750/year lost). Success = relaxed, fully booked, growing.

**10. DUNFORD — Positioning:** Don't compete in "SaaS" or "chatbot" category. Position in "más clientes" category. "Conseguimos más clientes para tu salón" is the frame she understands and wants.

**11. WIEBE — Voice of Customer:** Best copy is your customer's own words. Mine WhatsApp messages, reviews, interviews. "Ya no me preocupa perder llamadas" from a real client beats any clever headline.

**12. LAJA — CRO:** Conversion blocked by Friction and Anxiety. Salon owner's anxieties: "Will this confuse my clients?" "Will I lose control?" "What if it breaks?" Address all three on the page.

**13. CIALDINI — 7 Principles:** Reciprocity (free calculator), Commitment (quiz before pricing), Social Proof ("salones de Majadahonda ya usan Floux"), Authority (data + founder story), Liking (Max is local, not Silicon Valley), Scarcity ("1 salon per municipality"), Unity ("somos de aquí").

**14. KAHNEMAN — Loss Aversion:** Losses feel 2x more painful than gains feel good. "Cada llamada perdida son €50 que se van" > "Recupera €50 por llamada." Free trials exploit loss aversion — canceling after 30 days feels like a loss.

**15. FOGG — Behavior Model:** Behavior = Motivation + Ability + Prompt. Floux: Motivation HIGH (daily pain). Ability LOW ("setup" sounds technical — fix: "en 48h está funcionando, tú no haces nada"). Prompt WEAK — one dominant CTA per page.

**16. JTBD — Christensen:** Functional job: "help me not lose clients." Emotional job: "help me feel less stressed." Social job: "help me look modern without becoming technical." Market to the social/emotional job.

**17. LOCHHEAD — Category Design:** Create the category "Recuperación de clientes para salones." Own the language. Lightning strike: "El primer sistema en España que convierte cada llamada perdida en una cita confirmada."

**18. BERGER — STEPPS:** Social Currency (owner looks modern), Trigger (every missed call = think Floux), Emotion (anxiety of losing clients), Public (WhatsApp replies visible to clients), Practical Value (free calculator gets shared), Stories (Max walking Madrid streets).

**19. BALFOUR — Growth Loops:** Loops > Funnels. Floux loops: Word-of-mouth (client refers colleague), Review (Floux requests reviews → better ranking → more inbound), Referral (happy client refers, both benefit).

**20. LUXURY PRINCIPLES:** Scarcity as strategy. Show the conversation, not just "we send WhatsApps." Experience > features. €97 is underpriced — never apologize for it. Setup fee framed as a gift, not a discount.

**21. ARIELY — Pricing Psychology:** Decoy effect: Básico makes Agente look like the smart choice. Anchor with Completo first. "FREE" is irrational — "alta gratuita" more powerful than 50% discount. "Menos de €3.25 al día" minimizes pain of paying.

**22. HOLIDAY — Growth Hacking:** Product IS marketing — every WhatsApp Floux sends is a Floux ad. Earned media > paid: one article in a local Madrid outlet = €10k in ads. Hijack existing channels (salon owner WhatsApp groups, supplier networks).

**23. CRESTODINA — Content:** Own the angle: "Cuánto pierde un salón de estética en Madrid por llamadas perdidas." Email/WhatsApp broadcast > social followers. Hub-and-spoke: one cornerstone guide + smaller pieces.

**24. ROSS — Outbound:** Seeds (referrals), Nets (content/SEO), Spears (cold outreach). At launch: Spears first. Cold message formula: one specific claim + one question, no pitch, no links, 5 sentences max.

**25. RACHITSKY — Early GTM:** Do things that don't scale. Install Floux yourself with first 10 clients. Activation > Acquisition. One client who uses Floux daily and recovers 15 clients/month IS your marketing.

**26. LEMKIN — SaaS Sales:** 10 clients who love you > 1,000 who tolerate you. Churn kills everything — fix onboarding and day-7/30 check-ins before spending on acquisition. Raise prices when you have case studies.

**27. EFTI — Startup Sales:** Follow up until you get a "no." Silence ≠ rejection — she's busy. Break-up email after 5 unanswered: "Voy a dejar de escribirte. Si alguna vez quieres recuperar tus clientes, aquí estoy." Respond to every demo request in <5 minutes.

**28. NARRATIVE TRANSPORTATION:** Vivid, specific stories suspend critical thinking. Not "visité salones" but "entré en un salón en Calle Mayor de Majadahonda, era un martes a las 11, el teléfono sonó 6 veces en 20 minutos. No contestó ninguna."

**29. WHATSAPP DATA — Spain:** 33M users. 98% open rate (vs 20% email). 45-60% CTR. Beauty = highest WhatsApp conversion of any industry. This IS the pitch: "¿Cuánto abres los emails de empresa? ¿Y los WhatsApps?"

**30. LUXURY LOCAL B2B:** Luxury aesthetic signals permanence (not a startup). Triggers aspirational self-concept. Reduces price sensitivity. Differentiates from every blue/white "tech" pitch she's seen. Rules: never look desperate, show don't tell, price is quality signal.

## CREATIVE TOOLS

**Before/After/Bridge:** "Estás cortando pelo. El teléfono suena. No puedes coger. Ese cliente llama a la competencia." → "Ese cliente recibe WhatsApp en 30s, queda con cita, tú ni te enteraste." → "Eso es Floux."

**So What? Ladder:** Feature → so what? → so what? → emotional outcome. "Floux envía WhatsApps" → "cliente no llama a otro salón" → "son €600 al mes que antes perdías sin saberlo." Use the bottom, not the top.

**10 Headlines Rule:** Write 20 options. First 5 are obvious. 11-20 contain the breakthrough. Never use your first headline.

**Specificity = Credibility:** "12 salones en Majadahonda, Pozuelo y Las Rozas" > "muchos salones." "Carmen recupera 18 clientes al mes" = irresistible.

**The Villain:** Not Booksy or Treatwell. The villain = the missed call itself — silent, invisible, daily drain.

## ICP DEEP PROFILE

45-60 female. 10-20 years in her craft — it's her identity. Skeptical of tech (burned before). Primary fear: losing control. Secondary fear: looking foolish in front of clients. Trusts other salon owners, supplier reps, local people. Decision: slow, needs social proof, needs to see it work.

**3 real objections:**
- "Lo pienso" = doesn't trust yet → more social proof, follow up in 3 days
- "No tengo tiempo para aprenderlo" = ability barrier → "Tú no aprendes nada. Nosotros lo configuramos todo en 48h."
- "Es caro" = value objection, not price → "¿Cuántas llamadas pierdes al día? A €50 por cliente, ¿cuánto es eso al mes?"

## WAVE 0 CHECKLIST (run before any creative work)
1. Awareness level? (Schwartz) — Floux buyers = Problem Aware
2. JTBD? Functional + Emotional + Social job
3. StoryBrand check — owner is hero, Floux is guide, villain is the missed call
4. Write 10+ headline options, never use the first
5. Run So What? ladder on every main feature
6. Purple Cow check — what's genuinely remarkable?
7. STEPPS score — Social Currency, Trigger, Emotion, Public, Practical Value, Story
8. Loss frame? (Loss is 2x more powerful than gain)
9. Specificity audit — replace every vague claim
